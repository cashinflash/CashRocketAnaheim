How we protect your personal and financial information — and how you can protect yourself.

Tips to Protect Yourself from Fraud & Identity Theft

While we work hard to keep your information safe, there are steps you can take to further protect yourself:

  • Never share personal information carelessly. Do not provide your Social Security Number, bank account details, or other sensitive information online or over the phone unless you have verified that you are working with a legitimate company. When in doubt, call the company back using a number from their official website or your account statement.
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests. If you receive an email, text, or phone call demanding immediate action or threatening consequences, it is likely a scam. Legitimate companies — including Cash Rocket Anaheim — will never pressure you to share sensitive information through unsolicited messages.
  • Verify lender licenses. When applying for a loan online, make sure the lender is properly licensed. Legitimate lenders display their state licenses on their website. You can verify Cash Rocket Anaheim’s license on the California DFPI website.
  • Keep your software up to date. Ensure your anti-virus, anti-malware, and operating system software are current on all your devices. Enable automatic updates when possible.
  • Monitor your accounts regularly. Check your bank, credit card, and debit card statements frequently for unauthorized transactions. Report any suspicious activity to your financial institution immediately.
  • Use strong, unique passwords. Create different passwords for each of your online accounts and avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or names. Consider using a reputable password manager.
  • Enable two-factor authentication. Where available, turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) for an extra layer of security on your accounts.

What to Do If You Suspect Fraud

If you believe your Cash Rocket Anaheim account or personal information has been compromised, take these steps immediately:

  • Contact us right away at (747) 270-7121 or email security@cashrocketanaheim.com
  • Place a fraud alert on your credit reports by contacting one of the three major credit bureaus: Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion
  • File a report with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at IdentityTheft.gov
  • File a police report with your local law enforcement agency
  • Monitor your credit reports closely for any unauthorized accounts or inquiries

Report a Security Vulnerability

If you discover a potential security vulnerability on our website, we encourage you to report it responsibly. Please contact our security team and we will investigate promptly.
