How much can I borrow with a payday loan in California?▾

Under California's Deferred Deposit Transaction Law, the maximum face amount of a payday loan is $300, and the maximum amount you receive as cash is $255. The $45 difference is the lender fee. Your exact approved amount depends on your income and repayment ability.

What's the longest term you can write?▾

California law caps payday loan terms at 31 days. Most loans are written to your next scheduled payday, typically 14 days out. Your due date is clearly disclosed on the loan agreement before you sign.

Are Cash Rocket Anaheim loans CDFPI-regulated?▾

Yes. All loans are originated and serviced by Dhan Corp, a California-licensed deferred deposit originator (CDFPI license #214840). You can verify our license with the California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation at dfpi.ca.gov.

What are the fees for a payday loan?▾

California caps the fee at 15% of the face amount, so a $300 face loan carries a $45 fee and you receive $255 in cash. On a 14-day term, that works out to a 460% APR. See our Rates and Fees page for the full disclosure.

How quickly will I receive my funds?▾

If approved, you can often receive your funds the same day. In-store applicants may receive cash immediately. Online applicants typically get direct deposit by the next business day.

Can I apply with bad credit?▾

Yes. Payday loan applications are reviewed using multiple factors, not just your credit score. Your income, employment history, and ability to repay are all considered during the approval process.

How do I repay my payday loan?▾

Your payday loan is typically due on your next payday. You can repay using automatic bank withdrawal, a debit card payment, or another option agreed at signing. We review every repayment option with you before you finalize the loan.

Can I get a payday loan online?▾

Absolutely. You can apply online from your phone or computer in about 5 minutes. Once approved, funds are deposited directly into your bank account.
